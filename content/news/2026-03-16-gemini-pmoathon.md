## From Theory to Practice

On March 11-12, 2026, the OCT Project Management Office hosted the **Gemini PMOathon** — a two-day event specifically designed to build institutional proficiency in Google Gemini and NotebookLM across the branch.

This wasn't a theoretical overview of AI. The focus was squarely on **prompt engineering** — the practical skill of crafting effective instructions to get useful, reliable output from generative AI tools.

### What Participants Learned

The PMOathon was structured around real work scenarios that OCT staff encounter daily:

- **Synthesizing large documents** — Using Gemini to quickly extract key findings from lengthy reports, audit documents, and vendor proposals
- **Drafting professional communications** — Leveraging AI to create first drafts of briefing notes, project status updates, and stakeholder communications
- **Analyzing data with NotebookLM** — Uploading project documents and using conversational queries to surface insights and connections

### Why Prompt Engineering Matters

The quality of AI output depends almost entirely on the quality of the input. A vague prompt produces a vague response. A well-structured prompt that includes context, constraints, and a clear desired outcome produces output that can genuinely accelerate work.

Staff who attended the PMOathon left with:

- A practical understanding of how to structure prompts for different use cases
- Hands-on experience with Gemini and NotebookLM in a supported environment
- Confidence to experiment with AI tools in their daily project management work

### A Proactive Approach to Emerging Technology

The PMOathon reflects OCT's commitment to moving beyond theoretical interest in AI toward **practical, embedded adoption**. Rather than waiting for top-down mandates, the PMO created a grassroots learning opportunity that met staff where they are.

This is the kind of initiative that builds real institutional capability — not through policy documents, but through hands-on skill development that staff can immediately apply.

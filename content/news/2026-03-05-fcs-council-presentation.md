## Representing OCT at Council

On March 5, 2026, Acting Branch Manager **Nisreen Hussain** presented the Open City & Technology branch overview and performance measures to City Council as part of the Financial and Corporate Services (FCS) departmental presentation.

This presentation was a key milestone under the **new live inquiry format**, which replaced the previous written Q&A process for budget and departmental reviews.

### What Was Covered

The presentation provided Council with a comprehensive view of OCT's operations, including:

- **Branch structure and mandate** — An overview of how OCT is organized and the services it provides to the corporation
- **Performance measures** — Quantifiable metrics on service delivery, project completion, and partner satisfaction
- **Strategic priorities** — The key initiatives driving the branch's work in 2026 and into the 2027-2030 planning cycle

### The Importance of Live Format

The shift from written Q&A to live inquiry represents a significant change in how the branch interacts with elected officials. In this format, Council members can ask follow-up questions in real time, probe specific areas of interest, and request clarification on the spot.

This means that OCT leadership needs to be deeply familiar with every aspect of the branch's operations — from high-level strategy to individual project status. It also highlights the importance of clear internal documentation and communication, because the information that surfaces in a Council chamber originates in team-level work.

### A Team Effort

While Nisreen led the presentation, the content reflected the collective work of every section and team within OCT. Performance measures don't appear from nowhere — they're built from the daily efforts of staff delivering services, completing projects, and supporting municipal partners.

The successful Council presentation is a reminder that the work each team member does contributes directly to how the branch is perceived and evaluated by the City's decision-makers.

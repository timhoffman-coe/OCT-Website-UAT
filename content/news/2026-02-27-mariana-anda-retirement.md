## A Legacy of High-Impact Work

On February 27, 2026, the Open City & Technology branch celebrated the retirement of **Mariana Anda** after an 18-year career with the City of Edmonton.

Mariana's contributions to OCT have been described as truly transformative. Her work touched some of the most complex and consequential technology projects the branch has undertaken.

### Key Contributions

Throughout her career, Mariana played a leading role on several major initiatives:

- **eServices Platform** — Helped shape the City's approach to digital service delivery for residents
- **PLI Tech Stream** — Led technology work for the Urban Planning and Economy department's Planning, Lands, and Infrastructure portfolio
- **Cross-departmental partnerships** — Consistently demonstrated how OCT's work directly impacts the services that Edmonton residents rely on

### What Made Mariana's Work Special

Mariana's career exemplified what the branch calls "high-impact" work — technology projects that don't just modernize internal systems but fundamentally improve how the City serves its communities. Her leadership on the PLI Tech Stream, in particular, showed how deep partnership between OCT and client departments produces better outcomes for everyone.

### Thank You, Mariana

Eighteen years is a remarkable tenure, and the expertise and institutional knowledge Mariana built over that time cannot be easily replaced. Her retirement is both a celebration of an outstanding career and a reminder of the dedicated professionals who make OCT's work possible.

We wish Mariana all the best in her well-earned retirement.

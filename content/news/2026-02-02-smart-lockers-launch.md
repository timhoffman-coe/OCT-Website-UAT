## No More Waiting for Delivery Windows

One of the most visible improvements to how OCT delivers technology to staff launched in early February 2026: the **Automated IT Parcel Delivery System**. Developed by the Asset Management Team with the assistance of Stephen Mundy, the system uses smart lockers installed at Century Place and Edmonton Tower to facilitate the secure pickup of technology assets.

### How It Works

The process is simple and designed around staff convenience:

1. **Order placed** — When a technology asset (phone, tablet, laptop accessory, etc.) is ready for pickup, the system sends an automated email notification
2. **QR code issued** — The email includes a unique QR code tied to the specific locker holding your item
3. **Scan and collect** — Staff visit the smart locker station at their convenience, scan the QR code, and the locker opens automatically

There are no scheduled delivery windows to work around. The lockers are accessible **24/7**, which means staff on flexible schedules or working non-traditional hours can still get their equipment without delay.

### Why This Matters

This project is a concrete example of how OCT is improving the everyday workplace experience. Previously, receiving a new device often meant coordinating with delivery schedules, waiting at a desk, or making trips to a pickup counter during limited hours. Smart lockers remove that friction entirely.

### Current Locations

- **Century Place** — Main floor locker station
- **Edmonton Tower** — Designated locker area

The Asset Management Team is evaluating expansion to additional civic buildings based on usage data from these initial locations.

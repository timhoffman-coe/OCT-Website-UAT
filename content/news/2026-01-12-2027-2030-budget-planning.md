## Setting the Course for 2027-2030

The start of 2026 marks the beginning of what leadership has called a "very big year" for Open City & Technology. As the final year of the 2023-2026 budget cycle, this year is defined by the intersection of closing out current commitments and launching the planning process for the **2027-2030 four-year budget**.

The new budget is scheduled for final Council deliberation in **December 2026**, which means the work of defining OCT's technology priorities, investment cases, and service delivery targets starts now.

### Building on a Stronger Foundation

OCT enters this planning cycle better prepared than ever. Over the past several years, the branch has invested heavily in defining corporate IT maturity through frameworks such as:

- **COBIT analysis** — A governance framework for enterprise IT management
- **IT Governance Audits** — Independent assessments of how technology decisions are made and tracked
- **Service delivery metrics** — Quantifiable measures of how well OCT meets the needs of its partner departments

This work has elevated IT operations from a utility function to what leadership describes as a **"foundational strategic partner"** for the City of Edmonton.

### A New Approach to Council Budget Review

A significant change in this budget cycle is the **removal of the written budget Q&A process**. Previously, Council members submitted written questions that were answered in advance. Starting in 2026, this has been replaced by a **live inquiry format** during Council meetings.

This change places a premium on the readiness and real-time knowledge of OCT leadership. It also means that every team member's work needs to be clearly documented and communicable, because any aspect of the branch's operations could come up during live Council discussions.

### The Spring Capital Budget Adjustment

As the last year of the current cycle, 2026 also requires a **Spring Capital Budget Adjustment (SCBA)**. The Branch Finance team initiated outreach to team leaders in early March for inputs. Notably, 2026 will feature only one adjustment period, with remaining transactions handled through the year-end administrative process.

### What This Means for Staff

The 2027-2030 budget will shape what OCT works on for the next four years. Every section, every team, and every project contributes to the narrative that will be presented to Council. This is the time to ensure that the work we're doing today is clearly connected to the strategic outcomes the City needs tomorrow.
